BCH Summary

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) was created in 2017 as a hard fork of Bitcoin (BTC) with the goal of increasing the block size limit from 1 MB to 8 MB. This allows BCH to handle more transactions per second and lowers the fees associated with those transactions. The fork was also intended to preserve the original vision of Bitcoin as a peer-to-peer electronic cash system, as outlined in the Bitcoin whitepaper.

Since its creation, BCH has been adopted by a growing number of merchants and businesses, with some even choosing to accept it exclusively over other cryptocurrencies. In addition, BCH has a dedicated community of developers working on improving the protocol and building new applications that make use of the blockchain technology.
